Eyewear

Eyewear refers to any type of accessory worn on or around the eyes to enhance vision or protect the eyes. This includes glasses, sunglasses, contact lenses, and goggles. Eyewear can correct vision problems like near sightedness or far sightedness, protect the eyes from harmful UV rays or debris, and improve overall visual acuity. Eyewear can also be a fashion statement, with a wide range of styles, colours, and materials available to suit individual tastes and preferences.

Different types of eyewear

There are several types of eyewear available for people to choose from depending on their specific needs. Some common types to buy eyewear online include:

  • Prescription glasses: Corrective lenses worn to improve vision.
  • Sunglasses: Designed to protect the eyes from harmful UV rays and glare from the sun.
  • Reading glasses: Used to enhance near vision and alleviate eye strain while reading.
  • Safety glasses: Protective eyewear designed to prevent eye injuries in industrial and construction settings.
  • Contact lenses:Thin lenses placed directly on the eye's surface to correct vision.
  • Sports goggles: Protective eyewear designed to protect the eyes during sports activities.

Importance of eyewear

Eyewear is important because it helps protect our eyes from harmful UV radiation, dust, and other pollutants. It also enhances vision and corrects refractive errors such as near sightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism. Additionally, eyewear can be a fashion accessory and a reflection of personal style.

Q : How to know the correct frame shape for my face shape ?

Once you know your face shape, choose frames that contrast with your face shape and size, and consider the colour and style that suit your personal taste and lifestyle. It's also helpful to try on different frames to see what looks and feels best while you shop eyewear.

Q : What are the different types of lenses for glasses?

A : There are several types of lenses available for glasses, including:

  • Single vision lenses: these have the same prescription throughout the entire lens and are used for one type of vision correction.
  • Bifocal lenses:these lenses have two prescriptions in one lens, typically used to correct both near and far vision.
  • Progressive lenses: similar to bifocal lenses, but with a gradual change in prescription from the top of the lens to the bottom, allowing for a more natural transition between distances.
  • Trifocal lenses: these have three different prescriptions in one lens, used for near, intermediate, and far vision.
  • Photochromic lenses:these lenses darken in bright light and lighten in low light conditions.
  • Polarized lenses: these lenses reduce glare and are commonly used for outdoor activities.
  • High-index lenses: these lenses are thinner and lighter than traditional lenses, making them a good choice for people with high prescriptions.

Q :Is it necessary to get my eyes checked regularly?

A : Yes, it is important to get your eyes checked regularly. Regular eye exams can detect vision problems and eye diseases early on, making them easier to treat and preventing potential vision loss. It is recommended to have an eye exam at least once every two years, or more frequently if recommended by an eye doctor before you buy spectacles.
